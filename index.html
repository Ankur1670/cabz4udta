Taxi for Sightseeing in Agra

Taj Mahal

Marvel at the grandeur of the world-famous Taj Mahal, a symbol of love and an architectural masterpiece made of white marble. This UNESCO World Heritage Site attracts millions of visitors each year and is renowned for its breathtaking beauty, especially at sunrise and sunset.

Agra Fort

Explore the majestic Agra Fort, another UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its impressive architecture and historical significance. This red sandstone fortress was the main residence of the Mughal emperors and offers a fascinating glimpse into India’s rich past.

Fatehpur Sikri

Discover the deserted city of Fatehpur Sikri, an architectural marvel and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, reflecting the grandeur of the Mughal era. The city boasts a collection of beautiful palaces, courtyards, and mosques, including the stunning Buland Darwaza.

Itimad-ud-Daulah's Tomb

Visit the exquisite Itimad-ud-Daulah's Tomb, often referred to as the "Baby Taj," renowned for its intricate marble work and beautiful gardens. This tomb is considered a precursor to the Taj Mahal and showcases fine Mughal architecture

Mehtab Bagh

Enjoy a serene experience at Mehtab Bagh, a garden complex offering stunning views of the Taj Mahal across the Yamuna River. The garden is a perfect spot for photography and provides a tranquil escape from the bustling city.

Jama Masjid

Admire the grand Jama Masjid, one of the largest mosques in India, known for its impressive architecture and historical significance. This stunning mosque, built by Shah Jahan, stands as a testament to the grandeur of Mughal architecture.

Akbar's Tomb, Sikandra

Pay homage at Akbar's Tomb in Sikandra, an architectural wonder that reflects the grandeur and legacy of the great Mughal emperor Akbar. The tomb is set in a large, serene garden and features a unique blend of Islamic, Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain architectural styles.

Chini Ka Rauza

Explore Chini Ka Rauza, the tomb of Allama Afzal Khan Mullah, a poet and scholar in the court of Shah Jahan. This lesser-known gem is famous for its glazed tile work and Persian-style architecture.
